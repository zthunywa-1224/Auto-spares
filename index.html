Engine parts

The engine is the heart of a vehicle, converting fuel into mechanical energy. Most modern cars use internal combustion engines (ICE), where fuel burns in a combustion chamber to generate power. Key components include the cylinder block, pistons, crankshaft, and camshaft. Engines can be petrol, diesel, or hybrid, impacting performance and emissions.

Crankshaft

The crankshaft converts the pistons' linear motion into rotational energy, driving the vehicle's wheels. Positioned in the engine block, it features main journals and crankpins, supported by bearings. A balanced crankshaft ensures smooth engine operation, minimizing vibrations. Its design impacts engine performance, efficiency, and overall power output.

Engine Valves

Engine valves are crucial components that control the flow of air and fuel into the combustion chamber and exhaust gases out of it. Typically made of durable materials like steel or titanium, they operate via the camshaft and spring mechanism. Proper valve timing ensures optimal engine performance, efficiency, and emissions.
